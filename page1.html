Shavers & Bair 2016: Hiding Behind the Keyboard: The Tor Browser

Introduction

  • Based on Firefox, Tor shields users' IP addresses for anonymous internet use.
  • Combines user-friendliness with effective anonymity, accessible to all.
  • Strength lies in its simplicity, making it one of the easiest tools for anonymous browsing.
  • Enables anyone with an internet connection to surf anonymously.
  • Emphasizes privacy, allowing users to communicate anonymously.
  • Makes user identification nearly impossible without extraordinary methods.

History and Intended Use of The Onion Router

  • Created in 2002 by the US government, Tor aims for unrestricted and anonymous internet communication.
  • Serves purposes like accessing blocked websites, providing anonymity for whistleblowers, and facilitating private conversations.
  • Despite initial government development, Tor is presently independent and welcomes contributions globally.
  • Decentralized nature allows ongoing improvements and ensures relevance.
  • Technology offering anonymity can be exploited for criminal activities, reflecting its dual nature.
  • Ironically, the US government, Tor's initiator, is researching methods to deanonymize its users.

How The Onion Router Works

  • Tor (The Onion Router) ensures anonymity using random relays and elliptic curve cryptography.
  • The route changes every 10 minutes, making tracking more challenging.
  • The analogy likens Tor to mailing an anonymous letter with layers of encryption.
  • Each relay represents a layer, and only the outer envelope can be unwrapped by each participant.
  • Data is decrypted at the exit relay before connecting to the user's target.
  • An incident in 2011 highlighted the complexities of Tor's anonymity when a user operating an exit node faced legal issues due to misinterpreted traffic involving child pornography.

Tracking Criminals Using TOR

  • Various government agencies are actively working to deanonymize Tor users for law enforcement purposes.
  • Successful tracking of Tor users often results from mistakes made by the suspects rather than direct compromises of Tor's security.
  • The FBI exploited a Firefox bug (CVE-2013-1690) to capture the true IP addresses of Tor users involved in a child pornography case.
  • The weakest point in Tor's anonymity is often the user, who may compromise their identity through misconfigurations or customization of the Tor browser.
  • Various methods, such as man-in-the-middle attacks and controlling entry and exit nodes, are discussed, but they require significant resources.
  • The primary goal in Tor-related investigations is to obtain the true IP address, enabling traditional investigative methods.
  • In cases involving victims, cooperation can lead to opportunities for tracking suspects, such as seeding e-mails with tracking codes.
  • Internal corporate networks have an advantage in identifying Tor users if the network is being used with Tor, allowing for potential association of users with specific actions.
  • The article provides examples of cases where suspects' mistakes, such as using non-Tor browsers, led to successful investigations.
  • Despite Tor's strong anonymity, successful tracking often relies on suspect errors, emphasizing the need for careful and secure use of the Tor browser.

Quintin 2014: 7 Things You Should Know About Tor

Tor is an effective tool for protecting user anonymity online. It has been shown to be resilient against attempts to compromise it, even by powerful adversaries like the NSA. Tor is used by a diverse range of people, including activists, the military, families, and journalists.

Despite initial funding from the US Navy, Tor is an open-source project with no known military backdoors. Operating a Tor relay is believed to be legal under US law.

Tor is designed to be user-friendly, with tools like Tor Browser Bundle and Tails making it easy to get started. Speed improvements have been made in recent years, and developers continue to work on making Tor faster.

However, Tor is not foolproof. Users can compromise their anonymity if they use it incorrectly. It is important to use the recommended tools and to keep software up to date. Users should also be aware of potential risks, such as logging into identifiable services over Tor.

Overall, Tor is a valuable tool for people who want to protect their online privacy. It is effective, accessible, and legal. However, it is important to use it correctly to avoid compromising your anonymity.
